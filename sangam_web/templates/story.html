The Devas' Curse

Our story begins in the celestial realms, where the Devas, or gods, once lived in absolute power and glory. But their good fortune was abruptly taken away by a powerful curse from the angry sage Durvasa.

The Quest for Amrita

Lord Vishnu's advice was simple, yet immense: the only way to regain their power was to drink Amrita, the nectar of immortality hidden at the bottom of the cosmic Ocean of Milk.

The Great Churning

The Devas and Asuras agreed to churn the cosmic ocean together. They used Mount Mandara as the churning rod and Vasuki, the great serpent king, as the rope.

Lord Vishnu's Support

When the mountain started sinking, Lord Vishnu took the form of Kurma, the giant tortoise, and placed the mountain on his back to provide stability.

The Poison and the Sacrifice

The first thing to emerge was deadly poison Halahala. Lord Shiva drank it to save creation, turning his throat blue and earning the name Neelakantha.

The Arrival of Amrita

The divine physician Dhanvantari emerged holding the sacred Amrita. The truce broke instantly and a fierce battle erupted between Devas and Asuras.

The Final Trick

Lord Vishnu transformed into Mohini, whose divine charm mesmerized the Asuras. They handed her the Amrita, which she distributed to the Devas.

The Legacy of the Drops

As Garuda flew with the Amrita, drops fell at four locations: Haridwar, Prayagraj, Nashik, and Ujjain. The Kumbh Mela is celebrated at these holy sites every twelve years.
